FAQ — Interactive Demo
What is a full arch dental implant? ›
A full arch dental implant replaces an entire row of teeth with a fixed, permanent prosthesis supported by 4–6 titanium implants. Unlike dentures, they never slip, require no adhesives, and function exactly like natural teeth.
Will the surgery be painful? ›
Dental implant surgery is completed under sedation. You will feel no pain and may sleep lightly through the procedure. Most patients have no memory of their appointment at all.
How long does the full process take? ›
Treatment ranges from a few months to about a year depending on your unique needs. Many patients can have implants placed shortly after their consultation. In many cases you can wear a temporary restoration while healing.
What does it cost and do you offer financing? ›
Cost depends on how many teeth are missing and what preparatory procedures are needed. We accept CareCredit® and Proceed Finance, with terms up to 96 months. Our team will walk you through all options at your free consultation.
Animation Guidance
Entrance

Sections fade up on scroll: opacity 0→1 + translateY 20px→0. Duration 600ms. Ease cubic-bezier(0.25,0.1,0.25,1). Stagger children 100ms. Never translate more than 30px.

Hover States

Buttons: background 200ms. Cards: translateY(−2px) + shadow on hover 200ms. Nav links: opacity 0.7→1 at 150ms. Video thumbnails: scale(1.02).

What to Avoid

No parallax on medical content. No looping text tickers on critical info. No pop-in modals on page load. No bounce easing. No spinning elements. Premium = controlled calm.

Brand Psychology

Why it works · Emotional triggers · Conversion principles

Psychological Architecture

Every pixel is a trust signal.

01

Color Psychology

Deep teal (#34647D) signals medical reliability and calm authority — used by Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic. Mid blue (#6795BD) activates conversion without aggression. Together: clinical-but-warm. Patients find this combination physiologically reassuring.

02

Typography Trust

Cormorant's editorial serif projects intelligence and permanence — the visual language of specialists. DM Sans grounds it in modernity. The pairing communicates: "We've been doing this for decades and we're at the frontier." Patients read this as expert confidence.

03

White Space = Safety

Generous padding signals the practice isn't desperate for patients. Scarcity perception raises perceived value. ClearChoice uses dense layouts; NoviSmile uses open layouts to signal a different category entirely — private practice vs. franchise.

04

Video Testimonials

Real patient videos (Isidro, Melissa, Michael) are the single highest-converting element on a full-arch page. They trigger mirror neurons — the prospect visualizes themselves as that person. 30–50% opacity background video creates atmosphere without competing with the CTA.

05

The Straight-Edge CTA

Zero border-radius on primary buttons signals decisiveness and clinical precision. Rounded corners = consumer apps. Straight edges = surgical practice. Differentiates from 99% of dental sites using pill-shaped CTAs. Subconscious cue: "This is serious."

06

Lowercase Urgency

"don't suffer any longer." is lowercase with a period. It reads like a doctor speaking directly — not a marketer shouting. Intimate and authoritative simultaneously. Triggers emotional resonance far more effectively than ALL CAPS urgency copy.

Creative Direction

Photography · Lighting · Composition · Mood · What to avoid

Photography Style

Patient Transformation Portraits

Before/after as emotional journey — not clinical comparison. The "after" shot: patient laughing, eating, or socializing. Warm ambient lighting. Natural moments over clinical neutrality.

Dr. Frank — Authority & Approachability

Shot in operatory or clean clinical setting. Scrubs or professional wear. 85mm equivalent focal length. Expression: confident, calm, present — not posed smiling. Thoughtful authority, not marketing glossiness.

Technology & Practice Spaces

Wide-angle shots of clean, modern operatories. 3D scanner, dental robot, digital impressions as hero-scale imagery. Cool ambient + warm-white accents. Equipment should feel precise and reassuring, never intimidating.

What to Avoid
  • ✕ Stock photos of "generic smiling models" — feels inauthentic
  • ✕ Oversaturated, filtered Instagram-style edits
  • ✕ Extreme close-ups of teeth or mouths without human context
  • ✕ Harsh operating-room lighting as primary hero imagery
  • ✕ Cartoonish dental icons or generic clip art
  • ✕ Purple, orange, or red as primary or accent brand colors

Accessibility Notes
Color Contrast

White on Deep Teal (#34647D) = 4.68:1 ✓ AA. White on Mid Blue (#6795BD) = 3.01:1 — large text only (18px+). Always verify at contrast-ratio.com before production.

Focus States

All interactive elements: outline: 2px solid #6795BD; outline-offset: 3px; Never use outline: none without a custom replacement.

Minimum Sizes

Body text 14px min. Labels 10px min with increased letter-spacing. No text below 10px. Use clamp() for fluid type scaling across breakpoints.

Alt Text & ARIA

All patient photos need descriptive alt text. Video testimonials require captions. Decorative SVGs use aria-hidden="true". Forms need <label> elements.
